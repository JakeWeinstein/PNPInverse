- Bulk (y=1): Dirichlet `ŌÜ = 0`.
- Electrode (y=0): **no Dirichlet** in the Stern-on branch
  (`forms_logc_muh.py:836` only attaches `bc_phi_electrode` in the
  Stern-off branch). The Stern Robin in ¬ß2.6 sets the natural BC.

Side walls (x=0, x=1): natural zero-flux for all fields (no Dirichlet, no
explicit ds term ‚áí `‚ąá¬∑n = 0` implicit BC).

**Electrode (y = 0):**
  c_i^ex(x, 0) = c0_i ¬∑ [1 + őī_i ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx)]      (BV-flux Neumann ‚áí source g_i^elec, ¬ß4.2)

## 4. Source terms ‚ÄĒ what we subtract from F_res

Pattern: each production residual `+‚ąę X ¬∑ v dx` requires source
`‚ąí‚ąę X|_{u_exact} ¬∑ v dx`; each production boundary `+‚ąę Y ¬∑ v ds`
requires source `‚ąí‚ąę Y|_{u_exact} ¬∑ v ds`. We use the divergence
theorem to convert weak-form flux terms into strong-form sources
on the interior (and inherit the natural-BC residual on the boundary).

### 4.1 NP interior source (i ‚ąą {O‚āā, H‚āāO‚āā, H})

The production residual contains `+‚ąę J_i ¬∑ ‚ąáv_i ¬∑ dx`. IBP gives:

  ‚ąę J_i ¬∑ ‚ąáv_i ¬∑ dx  =  ‚ąí‚ąę (‚ąá¬∑J_i) ¬∑ v_i ¬∑ dx  +  ‚ąę J_i ¬∑ n ¬∑ v_i ¬∑ ds(‚ąāő©)

At u_exact the interior strong-form residual is `‚ąí‚ąá¬∑J_i^ex`. Subtract:

  S_c_i(x,y)  :=  ‚ąí‚ąá¬∑J_i^ex(x,y)
  F_res ‚Üź  F_res  ‚ąí  ‚ąę_ő©  S_c_i ¬∑ v_i ¬∑ dx_q

with `dx_q = fd.dx(degree=SRC_QUAD_DEGREE)`. Initial candidate
`SRC_QUAD_DEGREE = 8`; final value pinned by the sweep in ¬ß7.

The ds(‚ąāő©) IBP byproduct splits as:
- Bulk (y=1): w-component of `v_i` test functions is Dirichlet-zeroed
  (`u_i,őľ_H` Dirichlets at bulk) ‚áí no ds contribution.
- Side walls: `J_i^ex ¬∑ n` ‚Č° 0 by ¬ß3.2 ‚áí no ds contribution.
- Electrode (y=0): handled in ¬ß4.2.

### 4.2 NP electrode boundary source

The production residual at the electrode is

  +‚ąę J_i ¬∑ n ¬∑ v_i ¬∑ ds(elec)  (IBP byproduct from ¬ß4.1, **not** explicitly written
                                  but built up by Newton from the weak form)
  ‚ąí‚ąę ő£_j s_{ij} ¬∑ R_j ¬∑ v_i ¬∑ ds(elec)  (explicit BV term, line :615)

At u_exact, the boundary residual is

  R_i^elec,ex(v_i) = ‚ąę_{y=0}  [ J_i^ex ¬∑ n ‚ąí ő£_j s_{ij} ¬∑ R_j^ex ]  ¬∑  v_i  ¬∑  ds

Subtract that integrand:

  g_i^elec(x)  :=  J_i^ex ¬∑ n |_{y=0}  ‚ąí  ő£_j  s_{ij} ¬∑ R_j^ex |_{y=0}
  F_res ‚Üź  F_res  ‚ąí  ‚ąę_{y=0}  g_i^elec ¬∑ v_i ¬∑ ds_q(electrode_marker)

**Mobility vs physical flux:** production uses the mobility form
`Jprod = +D ¬∑ c ¬∑ ‚ąá(...)` (no leading minus, see `forms_logc_muh.py:499`).
The physical Nernst‚ÄďPlanck flux is `N = ‚ąíJprod`. All `J_i^ex` in this
section are mobility flux. The natural BC at u_exact reads
`Jprod_i ¬∑ n = ő£_j s_{ij} ¬∑ R_j` (mobility flux equals stoichiometric
production rate at the surface), which in physical terms means
`N_i ¬∑ n = ‚ąí ő£_j s_{ij} ¬∑ R_j` (physical flux equals minus stoichiometric
rate, i.e. species are produced into the electrolyte at rate +s¬∑R when
s > 0).

With outward normal `n = (0, ‚ąí1)` at y = 0, the explicit hand expression is:

  J_i^ex,y(x, y)        = +D_i ¬∑ c_i^ex ¬∑ ‚ąā_y(u_i^ex + őľ_steric^ex)              (i ‚ąą {O‚āā, H‚āāO‚āā}; z_i = 0)
  J_i^ex ¬∑ n |_{y=0}   = J_i^ex,y(x, 0) ¬∑ (‚ąí1)
                       = ‚ąíD_i ¬∑ c_i^ex|_{y=0} ¬∑ ‚ąā_y(u_i^ex + őľ_steric^ex)|_{y=0}

  J_H^ex,y(x, y)        = +D_H ¬∑ c_H^ex ¬∑ ‚ąā_y(őľ_H^ex + őľ_steric^ex)
  J_H^ex ¬∑ n |_{y=0}   = ‚ąíD_H ¬∑ c_H^ex|_{y=0} ¬∑ ‚ąā_y(őľ_H^ex + őľ_steric^ex)|_{y=0}

(Implementation uses `fd.dot(J_i^ex, fd.FacetNormal(mesh))` and lets
Firedrake handle the outward-normal sign automatically; the manual
expression above is for derivation transparency only.)

`R_j^ex|_{y=0}` is **x-dependent** because ő∑_j^ex depends on ŌÜ^ex(x, 0)
in the Stern-on branch (¬ß2.4) and the cathodic factors depend on
`u_H^ex,recon(x, 0) = ln c_H^ex(x, 0) = ln c0_H + ln(1 + őī_H cos(ŌÄx))`.

### 4.3 Poisson interior source

Production residual on dx:

  +‚ąę őĶ_coeff ¬∑ ‚ąáŌÜ ¬∑ ‚ąáw ¬∑ dx
  ‚ąí‚ąę charge_rhs ¬∑ (ő£_i  z_i ¬∑ c_i) ¬∑ w ¬∑ dx
  ‚ąí‚ąę z_scale ¬∑ charge_rhs ¬∑ (ő£_k  ŌĀ_k(ŌÜ; c_dyn)) ¬∑ w ¬∑ dx

IBP on the Laplacian:

  ‚ąę őĶ_coeff ¬∑ ‚ąáŌÜ ¬∑ ‚ąáw ¬∑ dx  =  ‚ąí‚ąę őĶ_coeff ¬∑ ‚ąá¬≤ŌÜ ¬∑ w ¬∑ dx  +  ‚ąę őĶ_coeff ¬∑ ‚ąáŌÜ ¬∑ n ¬∑ w ¬∑ ds(‚ąāő©)

Interior strong-form residual at u_exact:

  S_ŌÜ(x,y)  :=  ‚ąíőĶ_coeff ¬∑ ‚ąá¬≤ŌÜ^ex
              ‚ąí  charge_rhs ¬∑ (z_H ¬∑ c_H^ex)
              ‚ąí  z_scale ¬∑ charge_rhs ¬∑ ő£_k  z_k ¬∑ c_k^ster,ex
  F_res ‚Üź  F_res  ‚ąí  ‚ąę_ő©  S_ŌÜ ¬∑ w ¬∑ dx_q

For our chosen `ŌÜ^ex = (1‚ąíy)(őĪ‚āÄ + őĪ‚āĀ cos ŌÄx) + ő≥ ¬∑ y(1‚ąíy) ¬∑ cos ŌÄx`:

  ‚ąā_x ŌÜ^ex     =  ‚ąíŌÄ ¬∑ sin(ŌÄx) ¬∑ [(1‚ąíy) ¬∑ őĪ‚āĀ + ő≥ ¬∑ y ¬∑ (1‚ąíy)]
  ‚ąā¬≤_x ŌÜ^ex   =  ‚ąíŌÄ¬≤ ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx) ¬∑ [(1‚ąíy) ¬∑ őĪ‚āĀ + ő≥ ¬∑ y ¬∑ (1‚ąíy)]
  ‚ąā_y ŌÜ^ex     =  ‚ąí(őĪ‚āÄ + őĪ‚āĀ cos ŌÄx)  +  ő≥ ¬∑ (1 ‚ąí 2y) ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx)
  ‚ąā¬≤_y ŌÜ^ex   =  ‚ąí2ő≥ ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx)

  ‚ąá¬≤ŌÜ^ex      =  ‚ąā¬≤_x ŌÜ^ex  +  ‚ąā¬≤_y ŌÜ^ex
              =  ‚ąíŌÄ¬≤ ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx) ¬∑ [(1‚ąíy) ¬∑ őĪ‚āĀ + ő≥ ¬∑ y ¬∑ (1‚ąíy)]  ‚ąí  2ő≥ ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx)

(Implementation: use UFL `fd.div(fd.grad(phi_exact))` instead of the
hand-derived expression ‚ÄĒ same result, less risk of transcription error.)

The ds(‚ąāő©) IBP byproduct splits as:
- Bulk (y=1): ŌÜ is Dirichlet (w-component zeroed) ‚áí no ds contribution.
- Side walls: ‚ąā_x ŌÜ^ex = 0 ‚áí ‚ąáŌÜ^ex ¬∑ n = 0 ‚áí no ds contribution.
- Electrode (y=0): handled in ¬ß4.4.

### 4.4 Stern Robin boundary source

The production ŌÜ-equation residual on `ds(elec)` is

  R_ŌÜ^elec(w; U)  =  ‚ąę_{y=0}  [ őĶ_coeff ¬∑ ‚ąáŌÜ ¬∑ n  ‚ąí  C_S^model ¬∑ (ŌÜ_app^model ‚ąí ŌÜ) ]  ¬∑  w  ¬∑  ds

  where the őĶ_coeff ¬∑ ‚ąáŌÜ ¬∑ n term is the IBP byproduct from the
  őĶ_coeff ¬∑ ‚ąáŌÜ ¬∑ ‚ąáw ¬∑ dx Laplacian term (`forms_logc_muh.py:644`) and the
  ‚ąíC_S^model ¬∑ (ŌÜ_app^model ‚ąí ŌÜ) term is the explicit Stern Robin
  (`:668`).

At u_exact, subtract:

  g_S(x)  :=  őĶ_coeff ¬∑ (‚ąáŌÜ^ex ¬∑ n) |_{y=0}  ‚ąí  C_S^model ¬∑ (ŌÜ_app^model ‚ąí ŌÜ^ex|_{y=0})
  F_res ‚Üź  F_res  ‚ąí  ‚ąę_{y=0}  g_S ¬∑ w ¬∑ ds_q(electrode_marker)

Concrete pieces:

  (‚ąáŌÜ^ex ¬∑ n) |_{y=0}  =  ‚ąí  ‚ąā_y ŌÜ^ex(x, 0)
                         =  ‚ąí  [ ‚ąí(őĪ‚āÄ + őĪ‚āĀ cos ŌÄx)  +  ő≥ ¬∑ (1 ‚ąí 2¬∑0) ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx) ]
                         =  (őĪ‚āÄ + őĪ‚āĀ cos ŌÄx)  ‚ąí  ő≥ ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx)
                         =  őĪ‚āÄ  +  (őĪ‚āĀ ‚ąí ő≥) ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx)

  ŌÜ^ex(x, 0)  =  őĪ‚āÄ + őĪ‚āĀ ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx)

  g_S(x)  =  őĶ_coeff ¬∑ [őĪ‚āÄ + (őĪ‚āĀ ‚ąí ő≥) ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx)]
            ‚ąí  C_S^model ¬∑ [ŌÜ_app^model ‚ąí őĪ‚āÄ ‚ąí őĪ‚āĀ ¬∑ cos(ŌÄx)]

**Sign check:** the production weak form has `‚ąíC_S^model ¬∑ (ŌÜ_app ‚ąí ŌÜ) ¬∑ w ¬∑ ds`,
so at u_exact the residual is
`[őĶ_coeff ¬∑ ‚ąáŌÜ^ex ¬∑ n ‚ąí C_S^model ¬∑ (ŌÜ_app ‚ąí ŌÜ^ex)] ¬∑ w ¬∑ ds`. Subtracting
this integrand cancels the residual exactly. ‚úď

(Implementation: use `fd.dot(fd.grad(phi_exact), fd.FacetNormal(mesh))` and
let Firedrake handle the outward-normal sign.)

## 8. What's deliberately deferred

- **Time-dependent verification**: this MMS verifies the steady-state
  spatial operator. Time-stepping is exercised by setting U_prev=U_manuf
  and dt = 1e15, so `(c ‚ąí c_old)/dt` is O(h¬≤/dt) ‚Čą negligible. A
  separate time-MMS would manufacture a c(x,y,t) and test temporal
  accuracy ‚ÄĒ orthogonal scope.
- **Adjoint Jacobian check**: a Taylor-test on the linearisation around
  u_exact would catch sign errors in the muh transform that are
  symmetric across the residual but break the Jacobian. Recommended
  follow-up, but not part of the convergence-rate MMS.
- **Clip-activation MMS**: mirror of `mms_voltage_sweep.py` adapted to
  muh + Stern + multi-ion; pushes V_RHE high enough to engage
  `exponent_clip` and quantify the convergence-rate degradation.
  Orthogonal to baseline-correctness verification here.
- **Bikerman-saturation regime**: at high cathodic drive the multi-ion

  `reversible=True, anodic_species=None, c_ref_model=1.0` would cover

- **Legacy per-species `bv_c_ref_model_vals` path** (`:617‚Äď636`): gated

"""Hybrid proton-electrochemical-potential transform for the BV PNP solver.

Experimental ``formulation="logc_muh"`` formulation.  Mirrors
``forms_logc.py`` but stores the proton (H+) primary variable as the
electrochemical potential

    mu_H = u_H + em * z_H * phi    where    em*z_H = +1 in the production scaling

so that ``c_H = exp(mu_H - em*z_H*phi)`` is reconstructed from the muh
unknown plus the resolved phi.  In Debye/Boltzmann regions ``mu_H`` is
nearly flat (``log(c_H_bulk)``), even when ``u_H`` and ``phi`` each vary
by tens of log-units, giving Newton a much smoother primary variable.
The Nernst-Planck flux for the muh species reduces to

    J_H = -D_H * c_H * grad(mu_H)

with no separate ``em*z*grad(phi)`` drift term.

Non-mu species (O2, H2O2 ‚ÄĒ neutrals) are unchanged from ``forms_logc.py``:
their primary variables remain ``u_i = log(c_i)``.

This module is a near-clone of ``forms_logc.py`` with substitutions on the
five ``c_H``-touch sites (NP flux, BV surface concentration, BV log-rate,
Bikerman packing, Poisson source) plus the IC pair.  The duplication is
intentional for the experimental phase; consolidation is tracked for after
Phase 7 (charged-species mu) lands.

Critical implementation notes:

* ``c_H_old`` for the time term must reconstruct using ``phi_prev =

* The ``u_clamp`` is applied to the reconstructed ``log(c_H) = mu_H -
