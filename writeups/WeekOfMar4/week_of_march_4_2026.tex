\section{Overview}
%% ============================================================

This report summarizes eleven phases of work on the Poisson--Nernst--Planck
Butler--Volmer (PNP--BV) inverse problem for a four-species electrochemical
system.  We describe the forward solver and its validation
(\S\ref{sec:forward}), the adjoint-gradient inference pipeline
(\S\ref{sec:pipeline}), and parameter recovery results for kinetic
parameters~$k_0$ and~$\alpha$ (\S\ref{sec:original}--\ref{sec:extended}),
Bikerman steric exclusion sizes~$a_j$ (\S\ref{sec:steric}), the
effect of symmetric (anodic + cathodic) voltage data
(\S\ref{sec:symmetric}), multi-observable joint fitting of total and
peroxide currents (\S\ref{sec:multiobs}), multi-pH inference
(\S\ref{sec:multipH}), forward solver robustness improvements
(\S\ref{sec:robustness}), the master inference protocol
(\S\ref{sec:master}), speed optimization of the master pipeline
(\S\ref{sec:speed}), and parallelism optimization achieving a
4.4$\times$ wall-clock speedup (\S\ref{sec:parallel}).  Three voltage
ranges are compared: a 10-point ``original'' range
($\hat\eta \in [-1, -10]$), a 15-point ``extended'' range
($\hat\eta \in [-1, -46.5]$), and a 20-point ``symmetric'' range
($\hat\eta \in [-28, +5]$) that includes anodic overpotentials.

%% ============================================================
\section{System Description}\label{sec:system}
%% ============================================================

The model solves the steady-state PNP equations with Butler--Volmer
electrode kinetics on a 2-D rectangular domain (width~$W$, height~$L$).
Four ionic species are tracked: O$_2$ ($z=0$), H$_2$O$_2$ ($z=0$),
H$^+$ ($z=+1$), ClO$_4^-$ ($z=-1$), with concentrations governed by:
%
\begin{equation}
  \nabla \cdot \bigl( D_i \nabla c_i + z_i D_i c_i \nabla \phi \bigr) = 0, \qquad
  -\varepsilon^2 \nabla^2 \phi = \sum_i z_i c_i.
\end{equation}
%
Two coupled BV reactions act at the electrode boundary:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{R1}: O$_2 + 2\text{H}^+ + 2e^- \to$ H$_2$O$_2$ \quad
        ($k_{0,1}$, $\alpha_1$, reversible),
  \item \textbf{R2}: H$_2$O$_2 + 2\text{H}^+ + 2e^- \to 2$H$_2$O \quad
        ($k_{0,2}$, $\alpha_2$, irreversible).
\end{itemize}
True parameter values: $k_{0,1} = \num{1.263e-3}$,
$k_{0,2} = \num{5.263e-5}$, $\alpha_1 = 0.627$, $\alpha_2 = 0.5$, and
uniform steric sizes $a_j = 0.05$ for all species.  The mesh is
$8 \times 200$ with grading parameter $\beta = 3.0$
($L_{\text{ref}} = \SI{300}{\micro\metre}$).  All quantities are
nondimensionalized; the observable is the total current density
$I = \sum_j s_j R_j \cdot n F \cdot 0.1$ (mA\,cm$^{-2}$), where~$s_j$
is the stoichiometric coefficient.

Figure~\ref{fig:iv_shape} shows the forward I--V curve, which exhibits
four distinct regions: onset ($|\hat\eta| < 2$), transition
($2 < |\hat\eta| < 6$), knee ($6 < |\hat\eta| < 12$), and
diffusion-limited plateau ($|\hat\eta| > 12$).

\begin{figure}[htbp]
  \centering

  \caption{Forward I--V curve for the 4-species charged BV model, with
           annotated regions.  The secondary axis shows the corresponding
           $V_{\mathrm{RHE}}$.}
  \label{fig:iv_shape}
\end{figure}

%% ============================================================
\section{Forward Solver Validation}\label{sec:forward}
%% ============================================================

\subsection{Voltage Range Study}\label{sec:voltage_range}

The charged forward solver was tested across a range of overpotentials to
determine the maximum achievable~$\hat\eta$.  With the graded
$8 \times 200$ mesh, the solver converges reliably to $\hat\eta = -46.5$
(corresponding to $V_{\mathrm{RHE}} \approx -0.50$\,V), spanning the
entire experimentally relevant range from onset through deep plateau.

\subsection{Charge Configuration Feasibility}\label{sec:feasibility}

A feasibility study tested four charge configurations for the 4-species
system.  Only $\mathbf{z} = [0, 0, +1, -1]$ converges across the full
voltage range; configurations with charged O$_2$ or H$_2$O$_2$ fail due to
the extreme Debye length ratio $(\lambda_D/L)^2 \sim 10^{-10}$, which is
unresolvable on practical meshes.  The neutral-species assignment for the
reactive species (O$_2$, H$_2$O$_2$) avoids Poisson stiffness while still
coupling to the electric field through the supporting electrolyte
(H$^+$, ClO$_4^-$).

%% ============================================================
\section{Inference Pipeline}\label{sec:pipeline}
%% ============================================================

\subsection{Architecture}

The inference pipeline uses firedrake-adjoint to compute exact gradients of
a least-squares objective with respect to BV parameters ($k_0$, $\alpha$,
$a_j$).  For each voltage point, a steady-state forward solve is followed
by an adjoint solve to obtain $\partial J / \partial \theta$.  Gradients
from all voltage points are accumulated and passed to SciPy's L-BFGS-B
optimizer.  The $k_0$ values are optimized in $\log_{10}$ space (with
appropriate chain-rule correction) while $\alpha$ and steric~$a$ remain in
linear space.

Warm-start voltage continuation is used: voltage points are solved in
order of increasing $|\hat\eta|$, with each solve initialized from the
previous converged solution.

\subsection{Bridge Points}\label{sec:bridge}

For the extended 15-point range, consecutive voltage gaps can exceed
$\Delta\hat\eta = 3$.  Automatic bridge-point insertion fills these gaps
with forward-only (no adjoint) solves to maintain warm-start continuity.
Seven bridge points are inserted per evaluation in the plateau region
($\hat\eta < -13$).  Across all Phase~5 experiments, \textbf{zero bridge
point failures} were observed in 2046 total bridge evaluations, with
$\sim$10--15\% wall-time overhead.

\section{Inference Results: Symmetric Range}\label{sec:symmetric}
%% ============================================================

Adding anodic (positive) overpotentials provides an independent
constraint on~$\alpha$ through the $(1-\alpha)$ Tafel slope,
potentially breaking the $k_0$--$\alpha$ correlation.  A
\textbf{two-branch sweep} was implemented: the cathodic branch is
solved first (ascending $|\hat\eta|$), then the solver resets to a
saved ``hub'' state near equilibrium and solves the anodic branch
(ascending $\hat\eta$).  The symmetric range uses 20~points:
5~anodic ($\hat\eta \in [+0.5, +5]$), 2~near-equilibrium, and
13~cathodic ($\hat\eta \in [-1, -28]$).

Figure~\ref{fig:sym_target} shows the symmetric target I--V curve.
One anodic point ($\hat\eta = +5$) failed during target generation;
the NaN-skip mechanism excludes it from optimization.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.88\textwidth]{symmetric_target_iv.pdf}
  \caption{Symmetric 20-point target I--V curve, showing anodic
           (positive $\hat\eta$), cathodic, and bridge regions.
           The failed point at $\hat\eta = +5$ is marked.}
  \label{fig:sym_target}
\end{figure}

%% ============================================================
\section{Multi-Observable Joint Fitting}\label{sec:multiobs}
%% ============================================================

Fitting a single observable (total current or peroxide current) provides
only one equation per voltage point, leaving R2 parameters in the null
space of the Jacobian.  We implemented a \textbf{combined objective}
$J = w_1 \|I_{\text{tot}} - I_{\text{tot}}^*\|^2
   + w_2 \|I_{\text{pxd}} - I_{\text{pxd}}^*\|^2$
that simultaneously fits the total current density and the peroxide
partial current.  Because $I_{\text{tot}} = -(R_1 + R_2) \cdot
\text{scale}$ and $I_{\text{pxd}} = -(R_1 - R_2) \cdot \text{scale}$,
the two observables provide two independent equations for $R_1$ and $R_2$
at each voltage, breaking the degeneracy.  The run used the 15-point
cathodic range with joint $(k_0, \alpha)$ inference, completing in
${\sim}19$~min (23~optimizer evaluations, final objective
$1.251 \times 10^{-4}$).

\section{Multi-pH Inference}\label{sec:multipH}

The Mangan et~al.\ (2025) data spans multiple pH conditions.  We
implemented a \textbf{multi-pH inference} that fits I--V curves at three
H$^+$ bulk concentrations ($\hat{c}_{\text{H}^+} = 0.4, 0.2, 0.1$)
simultaneously, sharing $(k_0, \alpha)$ across conditions.  Because R2

\section{Forward Solver Robustness Improvements}\label{sec:robustness}
%% ============================================================

Target generation for multi-pH inference initially failed on 22\% of
voltage--condition combinations (8/36), primarily at elevated
H$^+$ concentrations ($\hat{c}_{\mathrm{H}^+} = 0.4$) and large
overpotentials.  Root-cause analysis identified three failure modes:
(i)~Debye-layer stiffness at high H$^+$ that causes DIVERGED\_DTOL,
(ii)~BV rate explosion at large~$|\hat\eta|$ that triggers
DIVERGED\_LINEAR\_SOLVE, and (iii)~warm-start failure cascades where a
single unconverged point poisons all subsequent points in the sweep.
The following fixes were implemented: mesh refinement to $N_y = 300$
with grading $\beta = 4.0$; smaller pseudo-time steps $\Delta t = 0.1$
with $\text{max\_steps} = 500$; softplus regularization of the BV
exponential; a denser $\hat\eta$ sweep (19~points) with
$\text{max\_eta\_gap} = 1.5$; and reduced SNES linesearch step via
\texttt{snes\_linesearch\_maxlambda}~$= 0.3$.  The L2 linesearch proved
more robust than backtracking (bt), which was counterproductive.
After these fixes, target generation converges on 100\% of
voltage--condition combinations (57/57).  The condition
$\hat{c}_{\mathrm{H}^+} = 0.4$ was reduced to $0.3$ for stable
convergence; $0.4$ remains intractable.

\section{Master Inference Protocol}\label{sec:master}
%% ============================================================

The master protocol underwent three major revisions (v1--v3), each
informed by failure-mode analysis of the previous version.

\subsection{v1 Pipeline (5-Phase) --- Failed on R2}

The initial master protocol used a 5-phase staged approach:
Phase~1 recovered $\alpha$ via symmetric voltage data, Phase~2
recovered $k_0$ from cathodic-only data, and Phases~3--5 performed
joint refinements.  Phase~1 achieved excellent $\alpha_1$ recovery
(0.03\% error), and Phase~2 recovered $k_{0,1}$ well (4.7\% error).
However, the $k_0$-only optimization in Phase~2 drove $k_{0,2}$ to
98.8\% error because total current density is insensitive to R2---the
optimizer minimizes the residual by effectively zeroing out the small R2
contribution.  This poisoned Phases~3--5, which could not recover from
the corrupted $k_{0,2}$ starting point.

\subsection{v2 Pipeline (4-Phase) --- Partial Success}

The revised protocol removed the $k_0$-only phase and replaced it with a
multi-observable joint optimization (total + peroxide current) starting
from cold $k_0$ initial guesses.  Phase~2 achieved R2 recovery:
$k_{0,2}$ error 9.2\%, $\alpha_2$ 8.5\%.  However, Phase~3 used
total-current-only regularization annealing, which destroyed R2:
$k_{0,2}$ diverged to 137\% and $\alpha_2$ to 90\%.  This confirmed
the ``never total-current-only'' rule: \emph{every} phase that jointly
optimizes all four parameters must include the peroxide observable.

\subsection{v3 Pipeline (3-Phase, Multi-Obs Throughout) --- Final}

The final protocol enforces multi-observable fitting in every joint
optimization phase:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Phase 1: $\alpha$-only symmetric.}  12-point symmetric
        range, $\alpha$-only optimization.
        Result: $\alpha_1$ error = 0.36\%.

  \item \textbf{Phase 2: Multi-obs shallow cathodic.}  10-point shallow
        range ($\hat\eta \in [-1, -13]$), multi-observable joint
        $(k_0, \alpha)$ from cold $k_0$ start.
        Result: all four parameters 8--18\% error.

  \item \textbf{Phase 3: Multi-obs full cathodic.}  15-point full range
        ($\hat\eta \in [-1, -46.5]$), multi-observable joint, warm-start
        from Phase~2.
        Result: improved 3 of 4 parameters.
\end{enumerate}

The shallow-then-full strategy avoids solver failures during early
optimization (when parameters are far from truth) and adds the deep
plateau constraint once parameters are in the correct basin.

Three key findings emerge from the iterative development:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Peroxide current is indispensable for R2.}  No
        total-current-only method recovered $k_{0,2}$ or $\alpha_2$.
        The peroxide observable provides the independent equation needed
        to disentangle R1 and R2.

  \item \textbf{``Never total-current-only'' rule.}  Every phase that
        jointly optimizes all four parameters must include the peroxide
        observable.  This was validated across v2 (Phase~3 diverged) and
        v3 (multi-obs Phase~3 improved).

  \item \textbf{R1 vs.\ R2 trade-off.}  Methods optimized for R1
        achieve $k_{0,1} < 5\%$ but $k_{0,2} > 98\%$.  Methods
        including the peroxide observable achieve $k_{0,2} < 10\%$
        but $k_{0,1} \sim 17\%$.  No method yet achieves $< 10\%$ on
        all four parameters simultaneously with 2\% noise.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Speed Optimization of Master Pipeline}\label{sec:speed}
%% ============================================================

The v3 master pipeline (\S\ref{sec:master}) was optimized for wall-clock
time without sacrificing accuracy.  Two changes were retained in v4:
(i)~shared forward solutions for multi-observable phases (the forward PDE
solve is identical for total and peroxide current, so removing cache
clearing between observables allows the checkpoint-restart fast path),
and (ii)~predictor validation in the warm-start solver (early revert for
bad predictor states reduces wasted SNES iterations).  Several more
aggressive strategies---fewer voltage points, skipping Phase~1, and
relaxed convergence tolerances---were tried and reverted because they
degraded $k_{0,1}$ error from 16.7\% to 52--58\%.  Further speedup
($>15\%$) would require parallel MPI point-solves or GPU acceleration.

\section{Parallelism Optimization: 4.4$\times$ Speedup}\label{sec:parallel}
%% ============================================================

Three rounds of optimization were applied to the v4 master pipeline
(1832\,s baseline), exploiting the independence of voltage-point solves
after the initial warm-start sweep populates the checkpoint cache.

\textbf{Round~1 --- Parallel Fast Path (v5, 2.0$\times$).}
After the first sequential sweep, all subsequent L-BFGS-B evaluations
solve each voltage point independently from cached initial conditions.
A \texttt{ProcessPoolExecutor} with \texttt{spawn} context (matching
the existing Robin pipeline in \texttt{FluxCurve/point\_solve.py})
dispatches these solves to worker processes.  A new module
\texttt{FluxCurve/bv\_parallel.py} provides the worker infrastructure:
serializable config, per-worker Firedrake initialization, and
forward$+$adjoint computation from cached ICs.  Phase~2 dropped from
696\,s to 346\,s and Phase~3 from 964\,s to 408\,s.

\textbf{Round~2 --- Multi-Observable Workers (v6, 3.5$\times$ cumulative).}
Workers were extended to compute \emph{both} adjoint gradients (total and
peroxide current) after a single forward solve, eliminating the sequential
secondary-observable sweep.  A ``two-tape-pass'' approach clears the
adjoint tape after each observable, rebuilds forms, and loads the
converged state for a single zero-residual SNES step before computing
the adjoint.  Phase~2 dropped to 168\,s and Phase~3 to 188\,s.

\textbf{Round~3 --- Optimizer Tolerances + Pool Reuse (v7, 4.4$\times$).}
Relaxed L-BFGS-B convergence (\texttt{gtol}$\,=5{\times}10^{-6}$,
\texttt{ftol}$\,=10^{-8}$) for Phases~2/3 reduced evaluation counts
(${\sim}40 \to {\sim}26$ and ${\sim}38 \to {\sim}15$, respectively).
Reusing a single worker pool across phases saved ${\sim}10$\,s of
process-spawn overhead.  Accuracy paradoxically \emph{improved},
suggesting the tighter tolerances had caused overshooting.

\section{Conclusions}\label{sec:conclusions}
%% ============================================================

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The 3-phase master protocol v3 recovers all four BV
        parameters to within 17\%}: $k_{0,1}$: 16.7\%, $k_{0,2}$: 6.1\%,
        $\alpha_1$: 8.2\%, $\alpha_2$: 11.7\%.  This is the first method
        to achieve meaningful simultaneous recovery of both R1 and R2
        kinetic parameters.

  \item \textbf{Parallelism optimization achieves a 4.4$\times$ speedup
        (1832\,s $\to$ 415\,s) with improved accuracy}: process-level
        parallelism of independent voltage-point solves, multi-observable
        workers, relaxed optimizer tolerances, and pool reuse reduce
        wall-clock time from ${\sim}30.5$~min to ${\sim}6.9$~min.
        All four parameter errors improved by 2.6--5.8 percentage points.

  \item \textbf{Multi-observable (total + peroxide current) is
        indispensable for R2 identifiability}: no total-current-only
        method recovered $k_{0,2}$ or $\alpha_2$.  Fitting both
        observables simultaneously reduces $k_{0,2}$ error from
        ${\sim}97$--100\% to 6.1\% and $\alpha_2$ from
        ${\sim}67$--90\% to 11.7\%.

  \item \textbf{Pipeline ordering is critical---``never
        total-current-only''}: every phase that jointly optimizes all
        four parameters must include the peroxide observable.
        $k_0$-only optimization on total current drives $k_{0,2}$ to
        near-zero (v1), and total-current-only regularization annealing
        destroys R2 (v2 Phase~3).

  \item \textbf{An R1 vs.\ R2 accuracy trade-off exists}: methods
        optimized for R1 achieve $k_{0,1} < 5\%$ but $k_{0,2} > 98\%$;
        methods including the peroxide observable achieve
        $k_{0,2} < 10\%$ but $k_{0,1} \sim 17\%$.  No method yet
        achieves $< 10\%$ on all four parameters with 2\% noise.

  \item \textbf{Shallow-then-full multi-obs is the best architecture}:
        Phase~2 (shallow $[-1, -13]$) avoids solver failures during
        early optimization; Phase~3 (full $[-1, -46.5]$) adds the deep
        plateau constraint once parameters are in the correct basin.

  \item \textbf{Symmetric (anodic + cathodic) data achieves the best
        $\alpha_1$ recovery}: 1.8\% error via direct joint inference,
        confirming the physics prediction about the anodic Tafel slope.

  \item \textbf{Forward solver robustness was substantially improved}:
        mesh refinement ($N_y = 300$, $\beta = 4.0$), smaller timesteps
        ($\Delta t = 0.1$), denser voltage sweeps (19~points,
        max\_eta\_gap~$= 1.5$), softplus regularization, and SNES
        linesearch tuning raised target generation convergence from
        78\% to 100\%.

  \item \textbf{Multi-pH improves R1 but is limited by solver
        convergence}: sharing $(k_0, \alpha)$ across pH conditions
        yields excellent R1 ($k_{0,1}$: 6.5\%, $\alpha_1$: 3.6\%)
        and partial $\alpha_2$ improvement (29.5\%), but the charged
        solver fails at elevated H$^+$ concentrations.

  \item \textbf{Regularization helps but anchors to the prior}: for
        $\lambda \ge 0.001$, recovered parameters reflect the prior
        rather than the truth.

  \item \textbf{Bridge points and two-branch sweeps work robustly}:
        zero bridge failures across 2046+ evaluations; two-branch
        sweep enables anodic data collection with hub-state warm-start.
\end{itemize}

\section{Next Steps}\label{sec:next}

\subsection*{Completed}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Multi-observable joint fitting.} \textsc{COMPLETED.}
        Fitting total current and peroxide current simultaneously achieved
        the R2 breakthrough: $k_{0,2}$ error 6.1\%, $\alpha_2$ error
        11.7\%.

  \item \textbf{Multi-pH inference.} \textsc{COMPLETED.}  Three-condition
        fitting with shared $(k_0, \alpha)$ achieved excellent R1 recovery
        and partial R2 improvement.

  \item \textbf{Master inference protocol v1/v2/v3.} \textsc{COMPLETED.}
        Three iterations of pipeline design converged on a 3-phase
        multi-obs-throughout architecture that recovers all four BV
        parameters to within 17\%.

  \item \textbf{Forward solver robustness.} \textsc{COMPLETED.}
        Mesh refinement, smaller timesteps, denser voltage sweeps,
        softplus regularization, and SNES linesearch tuning raised target
        generation convergence from 78\% to 100\%.

  \item \textbf{Speed optimization of master pipeline.} \textsc{COMPLETED.}
        Shared forward solutions and predictor validation achieved 11.7\%
        wall-clock speedup (2075\,s $\to$ 1832\,s) with identical
        accuracy.  More aggressive strategies were tried and reverted.

  \item \textbf{Parallelism optimization (v5--v7).} \textsc{COMPLETED.}
        Process-level parallel point-solves, multi-observable workers,
        relaxed optimizer tolerances, and pool reuse achieved a
        4.4$\times$ speedup (1832\,s $\to$ 415\,s) with improved
        accuracy (all four errors decreased by 2.6--5.8 percentage
        points).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Future work}

\begin{enumerate}\setcounter{enumi}{6}
  \item \textbf{Combined multi-observable + multi-pH + symmetric.}
        Multi-observable breaks R2 degeneracy; multi-pH adds
        concentration diversity; symmetric data constrains $\alpha$.
        Combining all three in a single pipeline could push the R1
        vs.\ R2 trade-off frontier below the current 17\% ceiling.

  \item \textbf{Neutral-species formulation for wider pH range.}  The
        charged solver fails at $c_{\text{H}^+} > 0.3$ due to
        Debye-length stiffness.  A neutral-species formulation would
        avoid Poisson stiffness entirely, enabling multi-pH inference
        across the full experimentally relevant range.

  \item \textbf{Gauss--Newton optimizer.}  Preconditioning the gradient
        to decorrelate $k_0$ and $\alpha$ could reduce iteration counts
        and improve conditioning of the joint objective.

  \item \textbf{Experimental data fitting.}  Apply the validated v3
        pipeline to real RRDE data from Mangan et~al.\ (2025), which
        provides both disk (total) and ring (peroxide) currents.

  \item \textbf{Bayesian uncertainty quantification.}  Replace point
        estimates with posterior distributions via ensemble Kalman
        inversion (EKI) or MCMC to characterize parameter uncertainty
        and the R1--R2 correlation structure.

  \item \textbf{Transient (chronoamperometric) data.}  R1 and R2 reach
        steady state on different timescales; fitting $I(t)$ provides
        temporal separation invisible in steady-state data.

  \item \textbf{MPI Ensemble parallelism for further speedup.}  The
        current \texttt{ProcessPoolExecutor} approach is near its
        ceiling (9~workers, ${\sim}40$\,s pool init overhead).  MPI
        Ensemble parallelism would require restructuring from a
        single-process to an \texttt{mpiexec} launcher; deferred to
        future work.
\end{enumerate}
